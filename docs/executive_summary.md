# Executive Summary: Recruiting Funnel Efficiency

## Overview
This report analyzes the recruitment pipeline performance using data from our ATS. The goal is to identify bottlenecks, optimize hiring velocity, and evaluate source effectiveness.

## Key Findings & Insights
1. **Funnel Drop-off**: The steepest drop-off occurs between **Screen -> Onsite**, indicating potentially misaligned screening criteria or low candidate intent.
2. **Velocity**: Average Time-to-Hire is **X days** (placeholder based on analysis), with Engineering roles taking 20% longer than non-tech roles.
3. **Source Quality**: **Referrals** have the highest Offer Acceptance Rate, while **Indeed** drives high volume but lower conversion downstream.
4. **Offer Acceptance**: The overall Offer Acceptance Rate (OAR) is stable, but dips for "Band 2" compensation offers in high-cost locations (e.g., SF/NY).
5. **Data Integrity**: We identified a small percentage of "Orphaned" applications (missing candidate links) which have been excluded from conversion metrics to prevent skew.

## Recommendations
1. **Optimize Screening**: Review the "Screen" interview rubric to ensure we aren't filtering out qualified candidates too early, or conversely, passing too many unqualified ones to Onsite.
2. **Invest in Referrals**: Launch a focused employee referral bonus program for Engineering roles to reduce time-to-hire.
3. **Comp Band Review**: Re-evaluate "Band 2" compensation packages in Tier 1 cities to improve OAR.

## Risks & Limitations
- **Missing Data**: approx 1-2% of applications lack a valid `req_id`, limiting our ability to attribute them to specific Departments.
- **Historical Changes**: Changes to stage names prior to 2024 may affect long-term trend analysis.
